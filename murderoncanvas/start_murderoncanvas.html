the framed message puzzle (evidence 5)

EVIDENCE 10
Jennifer noticed Amy's unconscious habit of touching her chin when she is in love

Take a look at the fonts and colours. Can you make a connection between the first line and the rest of the text?

Every line of the text is written in a different font, except for the first line, which is written in multiple fonts. What could this signify?

Fonts used in the first line are later reused in the rest of the text. What about the colours?

Look at the font and the colour of each word in the first line. Take out the sections of text written in the appropriate font and colour from the rest of the text. What do you get?

The solution to this puzzle is "When in love Amy touches her chin".

the art magazine puzzle (evidence 6)

EVIDENCE 11
Jennifer knew that Kylie was broke

Is there something in the text pointing to where you should search for a puzzle?

The painting shown in the first photo of the article is described as "puzzling". Look at the objects in the painting. The title of the painting is a bit unusual, don't you think?

The title of the painting has five words, and there are five objects in the painting. Is there a connection between the two?

Name the objects in the painting. For example, you can see a gong. Is there a word in the title similar to "gong"?

The word "going" contains all of the letters of the word "gong". When you remove all of the letters that both words contain, the letter "i" remains. Look for other connections between words in the title and objects in the painting.

The objects in the painting are a robe, a gear, a rose, a gong, and a turtle. Remove the letters of the names of these objects from the words of the title and find the remaining letters.

The remaining letters are E, L, I, Y, and K. Can you make a word out of these letters?

The solution to this puzzle is "Kylie".

EVIDENCE 12
Jennifer knew that Marvin had fake followers, and she informed him about it

Read everything on the page. Don't forget the comments section.

Many of the commentators comment on the posts more than once. However, one of them comments on almost every post on the page. Take a closer look at the name of this commentator.

Jesper Fernnik is an unusual name. If you scramble the letters, you will get another name, less unusual than this one.

Jennifer Perks is commenting on Marvin Thorpe's posts under the pseudonym "Jesper Fernnik". Do you notice something in common in all of her comments?

All of Jennifer's posts end with two emoticons with an X symbol in between. Do these emoticons remind you of something?

The emoticons illustrate words mentioned earlier in Jennifer's comment. What is the meaning of X?

There is always one word in between the two words illustrated by the emoticons. Therefore, X represents this word. Find all the words presented by X.

The solution to this puzzle is "I know about your fake followers".

the leaves painting puzzle (evidence 8)

EVIDENCE 13
Jennifer's message says: "It's over"

The painting contains various types of leaves that are distributed in grid fields. Can you find a reason for positioning the leaves in this manner?

All the leaves of a specific type form a shape. What do these shapes look like?

The shapes formed by leaves are letters. But what is the order of the letters?

What is the biggest difference between the given types of leaves? This will help you put the letters in the correct order.

The leaves have different shapes. They are simple or divided into multiple parts, thus forming lobed or compound leaves. How could this be connected to the order of the letters?

For example, the clover leaf consists of three parts. This means that the letter S formed by clover leaves is the third letter in the message. Find the order of the other letters.

The solution to this puzzle is "It's over".

the letter to Ryan puzzle (evidence 9)

EVIDENCE 14
Jennifer's hidden message says: "I want you"

What do you find to be the strangest thing about this letter? Why would Jennifer draw these random objects in her letter to Ryan?

There is something in common with all the objects illustrated in the letter. Can you find what it is?

All the objects' names are compound words. For example, there is a picture of a horseshoe, which is made of the words "horse" and "shoe". How can you connect this to the text in the letter?

The text contains the words that form the names of the illustrated objects. Is there something interesting about their position in the text? Don't forget that these words make compound words, so they always come in pairs.

Connect all pairs that form compound words in the letter with lines. Some of the words are used in more than one compound word. What do you get?

The solution to this puzzle is "I want U".
